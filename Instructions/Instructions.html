The experiment - in short:

  • This study consists of two parts.
  • In the first part you will be making binary purchasing decisions. This means you will be making decisions between product A or B. In total there will be 38 decisions.
  • In the second part you will complete a short questionnaire. Everything you fill in will be anonymous.
  • The products that you will observe are
    real
    and have been selected from the Dutch supermarket Albert Heijn.

Purchasing Decisions

  • The purchasing decisions you will make today are hypothetical. However, we ask you to please make the following decisions as if you were in a real supermarket.
  • If you would not buy any of the products for yourself, please imagine that you are buying the product on behalf of someone else.
  • Therefore, always choose the product that you would actually pay for.

Product Characteristics

  • Price: This is the cost you would need to pay for the product. The price is in €.
  • Carbon Labels: display the amount (in kilograms) of carbon dioxide emissions per kilogram of product. This represents the total emissions produced throughout the product's life cycle. (). The colors in the carbon labels reflect their carbon emissions relative to other products in their own category. Products with higher kg of carbon emission () have a higher environmental impact. Remember, the emissions data shown are actual figures from products available at a Dutch supermarket chain, reflecting their real environmental impact.
    • Higher amount of kg of CO2 → less sustainable.
